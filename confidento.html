TheConfidento — Cross-Platform Communication App

Overview

Building a communication platform from a blank canvas

TheConfidento is a private communication app designed for users who want more control, more expression, and more safety in their digital conversations. I led the complete 0→1 design process — from initial concept and competitive analysis through final handoff — in a 6-week sprint that resulted in a successful launch on both iOS and Android.

The core challenge of 0→1 work: you're simultaneously making product decisions and design decisions with no established patterns to reference. Every screen is a judgment call. The pressure is on the designer to be the product's memory and its conscience.

88% user satisfaction score at launch

The Problem

Crowded market, low trust — finding a reason to exist

The communication app market is dominated by giants with deep network effects. For a new entrant, differentiation can't come from features alone — it has to come from a distinct, well-executed value proposition that incumbents can't or won't offer.

TheConfidento's opportunity: privacy-first, expressive, and genuinely cross-platform in a way that didn't feel like a compromise. The design challenge was making these principles feel tangible in every interaction — not just in the marketing copy.

  • Existing apps treat privacy as a checkbox — users can't see or control what's collected
  • Expression is limited — most apps are text-first with emojis as an afterthought
  • Cross-platform experiences typically degrade one side (iOS users get worse Android builds)
  • Onboarding is universally painful — sign-up flows are designed for data collection, not user confidence
  • No clear definition of "done" in 0→1 work — scope management is itself a design skill

"I switch between three apps to talk to different people. I want one app that actually respects me and works everywhere."

— Target user, discovery interview

Process

6 weeks, zero drift — scope discipline as a design practice

With a hard 6-week deadline and a small engineering team, I prioritized ruthlessly. I ran compressed discovery (1 week), established the design language (1 week), then designed the core flows in parallel with engineering building infrastructure (3 weeks), and spent the final week on polish and handoff.

01
Compressed Discovery
10 interviews in 5 days. Competitive teardowns of 8 apps. Defined 3 core differentiators: privacy visibility, expression depth, and platform parity.
02
Design Language
Established visual identity: warm neutrals, expressive but restrained type, a distinctive green-teal "trust" accent. Built in Figma with variables from day 1.
03
Core Flows
Onboarding, conversation view, privacy dashboard, and expression toolkit. Designed iOS and Android simultaneously — every decision made for both platforms at once.
04
Polish & Ship
Motion design for key transitions. App store assets. Handoff with annotated specs, component library, and motion guidelines. Submitted week 6.

Solution

A communication app that earns trust through transparency and delight

TheConfidento's design centered on three principles: show don't tell (privacy as visible controls, not fine print), expressive richness (reactions, drawing, voice notes, expressive typography), and platform parity (the same design quality on iOS and Android — no shortcuts).

  • Privacy Dashboard: A dedicated view showing exactly what data is stored, for how long, and simple one-tap controls to adjust. Privacy as UX, not legal text.
  • Expression Toolkit: Beyond emoji — sketch reactions, voice-to-text with playback, and expressive message formatting that works without markdown knowledge.
  • Progressive onboarding: Account setup designed to build trust first, collect data second. Only essential info at registration; profile enrichment surfaced over time.
  • Platform parity: Shared Figma component library with platform-appropriate adaptations — bottom sheets on both, native-feeling navigation, zero "it's better on iOS" moments.
  • Conversation architecture: Thread-first design with collapsible context. Conversations stay organized without requiring users to actively manage them.

Results

Shipped on time, rated above expectation

TheConfidento launched on schedule, with no feature cuts and no crunch-driven quality compromises. The 4.5-star launch rating — achieved without a paid UA campaign — reflected genuine product-market resonance. Users specifically called out onboarding and the privacy dashboard in reviews.
