Saarthi — Patient Management Platform

Overview

Healthcare design where the stakes are highest

Hemophilia patients require immediate access to treatment during bleeding episodes — delays can cause permanent joint damage or be fatal. Saarthi ("guide" in Hindi) is a patient management platform built for the Hemophilia Federation of India to coordinate care, track patient histories, and enable fast emergency response across their network of treatment centers.

The project required deep empathy for three distinct user groups — patients, caregivers, and medical staff — and an unwavering commitment to clarity under pressure. Every design decision was stress-tested against the scenario of a caregiver in panic trying to find the right information fast.

90% user satisfaction score across all user groups
62% faster emergency coordination response time
3× user groups served: patients, caregivers, medical staff

The Problem

Critical patient data trapped in paper — with lives on the line

HFI's existing system was largely paper-based and phone-call-driven. When a patient had a bleeding episode, the treatment center had to manually search files for their bleeding history, medication tolerance, and nearest factor supply — often taking 20–30 minutes in the critical window.

For a condition where treatment should start within 2 hours to prevent permanent damage, this friction was clinically dangerous. Coordinators were working off spreadsheets that were frequently out of date, and patients had no visibility into their own care records.

  • No centralized patient record — data scattered across paper files and personal phone contacts
  • Zero emergency protocol — staff improvised during crises, leading to inconsistent response
  • Patients had no access to their own histories — had to verbally recount their entire medical background at every visit
  • No supply tracking — centers couldn't see factor availability across the network
  • Caregivers (often parents of children with hemophilia) had no support tools between appointments

"When my son has a bleed at school, I have to call three different people to figure out what to do. By then, the window has passed."

— Parent caregiver, user research interview

Process

Research in the field — hospitals, homes, and emergency rooms

I conducted contextual research across all three user groups, spending time at HFI treatment centers to observe real workflows, interviewing caregivers at home about their anxiety management strategies, and shadowing medical coordinators during a shift. Research was deliberately done in situ — not in a conference room.

01
Field Research
Contextual observation at 3 treatment centers. 12 interviews with patients, caregivers, and medical staff. Journey mapping for each user group.
02
Emergency Mapping
Mapped the critical path from bleed onset to treatment. Identified 7 failure points where information gaps caused dangerous delays.
03
Design
Designed three interconnected views: Emergency Dashboard (staff), Patient Profile (shared), and Caregiver Home (family-facing). Stress-tested under simulated emergency scenarios.
04
Validate
Usability testing with real medical staff and caregivers. Ran 3 rounds of moderated testing including a timed emergency scenario simulation.

Solution

Clarity by design — the right information, in the right hands, in seconds

Saarthi's design prioritized emergency-first information architecture. The most critical data — bleeding history, current medications, emergency contacts, nearest factor supply — is available in two taps, at all times, for all user roles. Secondary information requires deliberate navigation, keeping critical views clean.

  • Emergency Dashboard: Red-bordered alert mode activates on a single trigger. Shows patient critical info, nearest factor stock, and step-by-step emergency protocol in one view.
  • Unified patient record: Complete history — bleeds, treatments, imaging, factor consumption — accessible by both patients and authorized providers.
  • Caregiver home screen: Built for anxious parents. Large, readable text. Panic button connects directly to the nearest HFI coordinator. Bleed log takes 15 seconds to complete.
  • Supply visibility: Real-time factor availability across all connected centers — medical staff can redirect patients to the closest stocked location.
  • Offline capability: Core emergency information cached locally. Functions without internet in rural areas with poor connectivity.

Results

Real impact on patient outcomes — not just usability scores

Post-launch validation showed strong results across all tracked metrics. The 62% reduction in emergency response coordination time is the most meaningful — for hemophilia patients, those minutes translate directly to health outcomes. The 90% satisfaction score was consistent across all three user groups, a rare achievement when serving such different needs.

90% satisfaction across patients, caregivers, and staff
62% faster emergency coordination from first contact to treatment
15s time to log a bleed episode (down from 5+ minutes on paper)

"For the first time, I feel like I have control. I can see everything about my son's condition in one place, and I know exactly what to do if something happens."

— Parent caregiver, post-launch interview
