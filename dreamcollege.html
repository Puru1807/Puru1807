DreamCollege AI — Platform Overview

Overview

Making college counseling intelligent, personal, and accessible

DreamCollege AI is a B2B SaaS platform that puts AI-powered college counseling in the hands of students who can't afford traditional guidance. As the lead product designer, I shaped the end-to-end experience — from onboarding flows to the AI conversation interface — ensuring that the product felt trustworthy, warm, and genuinely useful.

The core challenge: students are making life-defining decisions. The AI had to feel like an expert they could trust, not a chatbot they tolerated. Every interaction needed to balance AI efficiency with human empathy.

90% of users reported trusting the AI's recommendations
63% lift in 30-day user retention after redesign
150K+ active users on the platform

The Problem

AI that felt like a black box — students didn't know whether to trust it

Early versions of the platform had strong AI capabilities underneath, but the interface gave users no window into how recommendations were generated. Students felt anxious making major decisions on opaque suggestions.

Drop-off happened at two critical moments: during onboarding (too many questions, low-perceived value) and after first AI interaction (no sense of why the AI said what it said).

  • High early drop-off — students left before experiencing value
  • Zero explainability — recommendations felt arbitrary
  • Generic tone — the AI didn't adapt to different student profiles
  • No progression feedback — users didn't know where they stood in their college prep journey
  • Mobile experience was an afterthought — most students are mobile-first

"I don't understand why it recommended these schools. How does it know what's right for me?"

— User interview, sophomore student, user testing session

Process

Research, frameworks, and iterating in the open

I ran discovery across 3 user groups: students (primary), parents (secondary influencers), and school counselors (proxy experts). We conducted 18 qualitative interviews, 4 usability tests on existing flows, and a competitive audit across 7 comparable tools.

01
Discovery
18 user interviews, usability tests, competitive audit. Identified trust, personalization, and progress as core UX pillars.
02
Framework
Mapped AI touchpoints where explainability mattered most. Defined "trust signals" as a design primitive.
03
Design
Redesigned onboarding (10 steps → 4), built conversational UI with inline reasoning, and created a progress dashboard.
04
Validate
3 rounds of moderated testing. Iterated on AI tone, reasoning display, and onboarding pacing until trust scores hit 90%.

Solution

Designing AI transparency as a first-class feature

The redesign centered on three UX interventions: progressive onboarding that revealed value before asking for effort, inline explainability that showed reasoning in natural language alongside AI suggestions, and a personalized progress system that gave students a sense of momentum.

  • Progressive onboarding: 10 questions cut to 4 high-signal prompts, with deferred detail collection. Completion rate jumped 44%.
  • Reasoning layer: Every AI suggestion showed a 1-line "why" in plain language. Trust scores increased from 54% to 90%.
  • Progress dashboard: Visual college readiness tracker showing completion across test scores, essays, extracurriculars, and financials.
  • Adaptive tone: AI personality shifted between "mentor" and "coach" modes based on student stress signals and interaction history.
  • Mobile-first redesign: Complete responsive rework with bottom-sheet navigation and thumb-friendly interaction zones.

Results

A platform students trust to guide life's biggest decision

Six months post-launch, the platform saw compound improvements across every tracked metric. The retention lift — from 23% to 63% at 30 days — had direct revenue impact, with a 2.4x increase in subscription conversions.
